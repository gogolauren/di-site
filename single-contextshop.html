Contextual Research -- Shopping Behaviour Analysis

Teamwork | User Researcher

This project aims to redesign physical shopping experience. To do that, we did 12 interviews in total, interpreted them into notes and affinity diagrams. Then we did consolidation and drew story chart according to design ideas.









spotlight

Work Flow

Contextual Interview -> Interpretation into Notes and Diagrams -> Consolidation -> Design Ideas -> Story Generation -> Visualization





interview & Interpretation

To learn about shopping behaviour, contextual interview is the best way to gather information. There were 12 interviews in total, and our team finished 3 out of 12. Within the 48 hours after the interview, we researchers have to translate the observation and conversation into notes and diagrams. Notes should capture all details during the interview, and each one should also be explained by itself. Diagrams usually include a physical diagram, a day in life diagram, a identity diagram, and a sequence diagram.







consolidation

After sticking all notes of 12 interviews (over 50 notes for each), researchers need to consolidate them into hierarchy.

  • Yellow stickers are notes
  • Blue and pink stickers are high-level summaries
  • Green stickers are categorizations

By collecting all 12 diagrams of each type, we must fullfill them into a consolidation. Take the physical diagram as an example. The consolidation includes:

  • Chunks of space;
  • Physical layout of a shopping mall;
  • Notes of interactions with user numeber (e.g. U05).





Story Generation

After a silent design session during which each researcher walks around all the consolidations and writes down design ideas (DI), we collect these DIs and categorize them. According to DI, each group choose some topics to further brainstorm on. During each phrase, a process check (positives+ & negatives-) between and within groups is highly recommended.






visualization

To better deliver DIs, researchers need to re-draw the stories into nice visuals.
